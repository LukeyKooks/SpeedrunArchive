SPEEDRUNNING HUB

Introduction

Speedrunning is the art of completing a video game as fast as possible. It blends precision, strategy, and deep game knowledge. What began as a niche hobby has become a global competitive movement.

Early Origins

In the 1990s, games like DOOM and Quake introduced early speedrunning culture through recorded demos, leaderboards, and community competition.

Modern Era

Today, speedrunning is a global ecosystem with livestreams, leaderboards, and major charity events like Games Done Quick raising millions worldwide.

Core Techniques

  • Sequence breaking
  • Glitch exploitation
  • Frame-perfect inputs
  • Route optimisation
  • Advanced movement mechanics
Every game has its own “fastest logic” — speedrunners break that logic.

Featured Games

This site explores three major speedrunning communities:

  • Boneworks — physics-based movement and object manipulation
  • Minecraft — RNG routing, crafting, and Nether optimisation
  • FNAF Security Breach — glitch exploitation and out-of-bounds movement

Psychology of Speedrunning

Speedrunning is both mechanical and mental. Runners perform hundreds of attempts, learning patience, consistency, and resilience through repetition and failure.

Every failed run is part of the path to perfection.
